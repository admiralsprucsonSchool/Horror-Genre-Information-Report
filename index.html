Horror Genre

What is Horror?

Horror is a genre which is designed to shock, scare and frighten the reader. It has many sub-genres like psychological horror. Horror piece can very in length a lot, from short storys to massive novels. Horror is a genre which has books targeting kids and others targeting adults. This website will be focusing on Coraline by Neil Gaimen in particular

The Beginning of Horror

Horror has its roots with the Ancient Greeks and Romans, as in their plays and texts they had storys about ghosts and demonic possesion, vampires and zombies. A lot of modern horror pieces are inspired by these ancient pieces
Fun fact: Mary Shelly's "Frankenstien" was based on the ancient story of Hippolytus, where someone called Asclepius gets ressurected from death

Common Character Tropes and Events

A common trope is blood to display crimes and bad things. Deaths are common as they are because it is "scary" to kill random throw away Character. They almost always leave it to the mddle-end of the book for the main character to actually realize that the person they think are good are actually evil.

Typical Horror Storyline

It starts with the character generally moving into some cursed house with some problematic thing with the main character reciving some weird item to use as a plot device later in the story. They get trapped in a parell world with probably "ugh" a twist villian which suprises the character and it always involves dead children for SOME REASON, ahem * fnaf * ahem. They think they beat the villiam and then it comes back and gets deafted really easily.

There are lots of examples of this, like F.N.A.F with lots of dead children and a character which "always comes back". And that is literally the plot of Coraline with the plot device, the twist villan, and the dead children. The Shining, a horror classic also falls under this with dead children and a haunted house, as well as the Haunting of the Hill House. Winne the Pooh: Blood and Honey also follows this cliché plot.

Horror Key Visuals and Conventions

The main images used in cliché plots is blood and gore, dead bodies, and screaming rich ladies. They have some person who doesn't believe in the thing, who dies almost straight away. They have some dark place or something along those lines with a body in the back. Scary music, or omimnus music is also played in the background to allude to something scary. This is not all of it but there is a lot of horror conventions which I don't even know.
